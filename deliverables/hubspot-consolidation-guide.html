The problem: The company runs 3-4 separate HubSpot accounts, one for each product line. When a customer buys Product A and later buys Product B, they exist as two completely separate contacts in two separate systems. Nobody has the full picture. Marketing sends duplicate emails. Service can't see cross-product history. Reporting is fragmented. And you're paying for the same customer's data multiple times.

The solution: Consolidate all portals into a single HubSpot Enterprise account using Business Units (Brands). Each product line keeps its own branding, emails, forms, and reporting, but everything shares one CRM database. One contact record per customer, with full visibility across all products.

The outcome: Cross-sell opportunities become visible. Customer service gets the full picture. Marketing stops sending duplicates. Reporting works company-wide. And you stop paying for the same contact 3-4 times over.

KEY BENEFIT

Every customer who buys across product lines currently costs you money (duplicate contact charges), time (repeated service conversations), and revenue (missed cross-sell opportunities). Consolidation fixes all three.

02 — HUBSPOT PLATFORM OVERVIEW

HubSpot is built as one platform with 7 products (called "Hubs") that all plug into a shared database. Here's what each one does:

SMART CRM
The shared database. Contacts, companies, deals, tickets — all in one place. This is the foundation everything else plugs into.
MARKETING HUB
Email campaigns, lead scoring, ads, landing pages, forms. Attracts and nurtures leads until they're ready to buy.
SALES HUB
Deal pipelines, email sequences, meeting booking, forecasting. Helps sales reps close deals faster.
SERVICE HUB
Tickets, knowledge base, customer feedback. Turns support interactions into happy, loyal customers.
CONTENT HUB
Website builder, blog, SEO tools. Manages your web presence with AI-powered content generation.
DATA HUB
Data sync, deduplication, warehouse connectors. Keeps everything clean and connected to external tools.
COMMERCE HUB
Quotes, invoices, payments, subscriptions. Handles B2B transactions directly from the CRM.
WHY THIS MATTERS FOR CONSOLIDATION

All 7 hubs share the same CRM database. When a contact interacts with marketing, talks to sales, opens a support ticket, and makes a payment — it's all on one record. This is exactly why consolidating into one portal works: one database, one customer, full visibility.

03 — CURRENT STATE: HOW MULTI-PORTAL BREAKS

Right now, each product line operates from its own separate HubSpot portal. Each portal is a completely isolated system — separate database, separate contacts, separate everything.

MEET CUSTOMER JANE

Jane is a real customer who buys across your product lines. Here's what her experience looks like today:

THE CURRENT REALITY
EVENT WHAT HAPPENS
Jane buys Product A Contact created in Portal 1. Deal tracked. Service team assigned.
Jane buys Product B (6 months later) Brand new contact created in Portal 2. Portal 1 has no idea.
Jane contacts Product B support Service rep in Portal 2 can't see her Product A history. Jane repeats everything.
Marketing sends campaigns Portal 1 sends Jane a Product A email. Same day, Portal 2 sends a Product B email. Jane is annoyed.
Leadership asks for customer count Portal 1 says 5,000. Portal 2 says 3,000. But 1,500 are the same people. Real count? Nobody knows.
SIX THINGS THAT BREAK
PROBLEM 1
DUPLICATE CUSTOMER RECORDS

The same person exists as 2, 3, or 4 separate contacts. Each version has an incomplete picture. No single source of truth about any customer.

PROBLEM 2
BLIND SPOT IN SERVICE

When Jane calls about Product B, the service rep can't see her Product A ticket from last month. She has to explain everything again. Slow resolution, poor experience.

PROBLEM 3
DUPLICATE MARKETING EMAILS

Each portal's marketing team operates independently. Jane gets bombarded with emails from multiple portals. She unsubscribes from one — but still gets emails from the others.

PROBLEM 4
NO CROSS-SELL VISIBILITY

The Product A team has no idea which of their customers also use Product B. "Customers who bought A but not C" campaigns? Impossible across separate portals.

PROBLEM 5
INFLATED COSTS

HubSpot charges per marketing contact per portal. Jane's record in Portal 1, Portal 2, and Portal 3 means you're paying for her three times. Plus 3-4 separate subscription fees.

PROBLEM 6
IMPOSSIBLE COMPANY-WIDE REPORTING

Revenue per customer across all products? Total unique customers? Customer acquisition cost? There's no single dashboard that can answer these because the data lives in separate databases.

04 — TARGET STATE: ONE PORTAL, MULTIPLE BRANDS

After consolidation, all product lines operate from one HubSpot Enterprise portal. Each product line becomes a "Brand" (formerly called Business Unit) within that portal.

BEFORE (MULTI-PORTAL)

  • 3-4 separate databases
  • Duplicate contacts everywhere
  • Deal history split across portals
  • Each portal sends its own emails
  • Per-portal reporting only
  • 3-4 separate invoices
  • Paying for duplicates

AFTER (UNIFIED PORTAL)

  • 1 shared database
  • 1 contact per customer
  • All deals on one timeline
  • Brand-separated email campaigns
  • Cross-brand + per-brand reports
  • 1 invoice
  • Paying once per person
HOW BUSINESS UNITS (BRANDS) WORK

Think of it as "departments within one office" instead of "separate offices."

SEPARATED PER BRAND
WHAT EACH BRAND GETS

Logo and colour scheme, email templates, forms, landing pages, subscription preferences (customers choose which brands to hear from), blog, reporting dashboards, marketing campaigns, and social media accounts.

SHARED ACROSS ALL BRANDS
WHAT STAYS UNIFIED

The CRM database (contacts, companies), deal pipelines (filtered by brand), ticket pipelines, workflows, users and permissions, integrations, custom objects, and cross-brand reporting.

CUSTOMER JANE — AFTER CONSOLIDATION
THE NEW REALITY
EVENT WHAT HAPPENS
Jane buys Product A Single contact created. Deal in Product A pipeline. Business Unit = Product A.
Jane buys Product B Same contact. New deal in Product B pipeline. Business Unit adds Product B.
Jane contacts support Service rep sees ALL of Jane's history — both products, all tickets, full timeline.
Marketing sends campaigns One subscription centre. Jane controls which brands she hears from. No duplicates.
Leadership asks for data One dashboard: true customer count, total revenue per customer, cross-sell rates.
CONTACT TAGGING

Each contact gets a "Business Unit" property with multiple checkboxes. Jane would have both "Product A" and "Product B" checked. This is how marketing filters campaigns, sales filters pipelines, and reports segment by product line.

CROSS-SELL UNLOCKED

With one database, you can finally run "customers who bought Product A but not Product B" campaigns. You can see total lifetime value across all products. You can identify your most valuable customers regardless of which product they started with.

05 — MIGRATION STRATEGY (6 PHASES)

Total timeline: 30-90 days from kickoff to full email volume. Each phase has a verification checkpoint before the next phase starts.

1
AUDIT & MAP
WEEK 1-2

Know exactly what exists in every portal before touching anything.

  • Inventory all portals: contacts, companies, deals, tickets, custom objects, properties, workflows, email templates, forms, lists, pipelines
  • Map property names across portals — same data stored under different field names?
  • Identify duplicate contacts across portals (export email lists, compare)
  • Document every integration connected to each portal
  • Document email sending domains and authentication status
WATCH OUT FOR

Properties with the same name but different field types (e.g., "Status" as free text in Portal 1, dropdown in Portal 2). These CANNOT auto-migrate and must be resolved manually before migration.

2
CHOOSE TARGET PORTAL
WEEK 2

Decide which portal becomes the consolidated home for all data. Three options:

  • Keep the biggest portal — Least migration work, most data stays in place. Best when one portal is clearly the largest.
  • Keep the best-configured portal — Cleanest data, best workflows. Best when quality matters more than size.
  • Fresh Enterprise portal — Clean start, migrate everything in. Most work but cleanest result. Best when all existing portals are messy.

Score each option on: record count, data quality, workflow sophistication, email domain reputation age, integration count, and team familiarity. Highest score wins.

3
SANDBOX TESTING
WEEK 3-4

Run a complete test migration in a sandbox environment before touching production data.

  • Create a Standard Sandbox in the target portal (Enterprise required)
  • Install the HubSpot-to-HubSpot Migrator app (by Datawarehouse.io)
  • Migrate one source portal into the sandbox as a test run
  • Verify: contacts, deals, tickets, associations, custom objects, properties
  • Test Business Unit assignment and brand-filtered lists
  • Test email sending from each brand domain
  • Document everything that doesn't migrate automatically
WHY SANDBOX FIRST

If something goes wrong in a sandbox, nothing is affected. If something goes wrong in production, you're dealing with broken customer data, missed emails, and angry teams. Always test first.

4
PRE-MIGRATION PREP
WEEK 4-5

Set up everything in the target portal that can't be auto-migrated.

  • Create Business Units — one per product line
  • Configure email sending domains for all brands (DKIM, SPF, DMARC)
  • Rebuild reports, dashboards, knowledge base articles, chatflows, playbooks
  • Set up teams and permissions (who can see what)
  • Install deduplication tool (Insycle or Koalify)
  • Export safety backups from ALL source portals (CSV)
  • Freeze source portals — no new records from this point
CRITICAL

Add email domains to the new portal BEFORE removing them from old portals. Both can be authenticated simultaneously. Removing a domain before the new portal is ready means emails will go to spam.

5
EXECUTE MIGRATION
WEEK 5-6

Move all data from source portals into the target. Migrate one portal at a time, verify, then move to the next.

  • Run migration tool for Portal 1 → Target. Verify record counts. Spot-check 20+ records.
  • Run migration tool for Portal 2 → Target. Verify. Spot-check.
  • Run migration tool for Portal 3 → Target. Verify. Spot-check.
  • Run deduplication — merge duplicate contacts (email match) and companies (domain match)
  • Assign Business Unit tags to all migrated records
  • Reconnect integrations to target portal
  • Invite users and assign seats/teams
6
EMAIL WARM-UP & GO-LIVE
WEEK 6-8

Do NOT blast full email volume on day one. Email providers need to see a gradual increase from the new sending source.

WEEK WHO TO EMAIL VOLUME
Week 1 Contacts who opened/clicked in last 30 days ~25%
Week 2 Expand to 60-day engaged contacts ~50%
Week 3 Expand to 90-day engaged contacts ~75%
Week 4 Full contact list 100%
KEEP OLD PORTALS FOR 90 DAYS

Don't delete source portals. Keep them read-only for 90 days as a reference and safety net. After 90 days, downgrade to free tier to stop licensing charges while preserving the data.

06 — WHAT MIGRATES VS. WHAT DOESN'T
AUTO-MIGRATES
HANDLED BY THE MIGRATION TOOL

Contacts, Companies, Deals, Tickets, Custom Objects, Properties (all custom fields), Associations (all links between records), Lists, Forms (structure only), Workflows, Marketing Emails, Pipelines, Users/Owners, Engagements (meetings, tasks, notes, calls, emails, SMS), Files & Attachments, Line Items & Products, Currencies, Deal Splits.

MUST BE MANUALLY RECREATED
REBUILD THESE IN THE TARGET PORTAL

Reports & Dashboards, CTAs, Landing Page Designs, Website Pages, Blog Posts, Email Template Designs, Knowledge Base Articles, Chatflows, Meeting Links, Custom Surveys, Brand Kits, Playbooks, Snippets, Saved Views, Team Structure, Permissions Configuration.

LOST PERMANENTLY
CANNOT BE MIGRATED OR RECREATED

Marketing email campaign performance data (historical open/click rates), Historical web traffic analytics, Properties with the same name but different field types across portals (must resolve before migration).

PLANNING TIP

Screenshot or export your best-performing email campaign data before migration. The campaigns themselves migrate, but their performance metrics don't. Keep records of your top subject lines, open rates, and click rates for reference.

07 — EMAIL DOMAIN PROTECTION

Email is the highest-risk area of this migration. Get it wrong and your emails go to spam. Here's how to protect your sending reputation.

PER-DOMAIN SETUP (REPEAT FOR EACH BRAND)
STEP ACTION WHAT IT DOES
1 Add sending domain in target portal Tells HubSpot "I want to send email from this domain"
2 Create 2 DKIM CNAME records in DNS Proves emails actually come from your domain (not spoofed)
3 Add HubSpot to existing SPF record Authorises HubSpot's servers to send on your behalf
4 Verify DMARC policy Tells email providers what to do with emails that fail checks
5 Wait for DNS propagation (10 min - 48 hrs) DNS records spread across the internet
6 Send test emails to Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo Verify delivery + check headers for authentication pass
7 Begin warm-up schedule Gradually increase volume over 4 weeks
NEVER DO THIS

Never remove a domain from the old portal before it's fully set up and warmed up on the new portal. Both portals can have the same domain authenticated simultaneously — this is safe and expected during transition.

SPF RECORD WARNING

You can only have ONE SPF record per domain. Don't create a new one — add include:hubspot.com to your existing SPF record. Creating a duplicate SPF record will break email authentication for ALL your email, not just HubSpot.

08 — USER & SEAT MIGRATION

Every person who uses HubSpot needs a "seat" — this is their access pass. Different seat types give access to different tools.

SEAT TYPES EXPLAINED
SEAT TYPE WHAT IT GIVES ACCESS TO WHO NEEDS IT
Core Seat Basic CRM access — view/edit contacts, companies, deals Everyone who uses HubSpot
Sales Seat Core + Sales Hub tools (sequences, forecasting, quotes) Sales reps and sales managers
Service Seat Core + Service Hub tools (tickets, knowledge base, SLAs) Support reps and service managers
THREE APPROACHES
RECOMMENDED
OPTION 1: INVITE USERS TO NEW PORTAL

Invite the same email addresses to the target portal. Assign seats. Configure permissions. Simplest approach — everyone gets a fresh login to the consolidated system.

ALTERNATIVE
OPTION 2: TRANSFER SEATS

If keeping an existing portal as the target, transfer core seats between users. Good when the target portal already has most of the right people.

OPPORTUNITY
OPTION 3: RESTRUCTURE TEAMS

Use migration as an opportunity to redesign teams and permissions around Business Units. Create team structures that match the new brand-based organisation. Takes more planning but results in a cleaner setup.

09 — RISK REGISTER
RISK LIKELIHOOD IMPACT MITIGATION
Duplicate contacts not fully resolved HIGH HIGH Run dedup tool before AND after migration. Use fuzzy matching (name + company + phone), not just email.
Email reputation drops (spam) MEDIUM CRITICAL Follow 4-week warm-up strictly. Start with engaged contacts only. Monitor daily.
Workflows fire incorrectly MEDIUM HIGH Migrate all workflows PAUSED. Activate one by one after testing with sample records.
Property type conflicts MEDIUM HIGH Complete property mapping table before migration. Resolve all conflicts in advance.
Integration failures MEDIUM MEDIUM Document all integrations. Reconnect and test each one. Allow 1-2 days for debugging.
Lost deal/ticket associations LOW CRITICAL Verify association counts pre vs post migration. Spot-check 20+ random records.
User adoption resistance MEDIUM MEDIUM Training before go-live. Per-team "where to find your stuff" guides. Migration champion per team.
Temporary cost spike HIGH LOW Budget for 2-3 months of overlap (old + new portals). Downgrade old portals after 90-day hold.
ROLLBACK PLAN

If migration goes critically wrong: stop all activity in the target portal, re-activate source portals, resume operations from source portals, diagnose the issue, fix in sandbox, and retry. This is why source portals are kept read-only for 90 days — they're your safety net.

11 — SANDBOX & TESTING

HubSpot Enterprise includes a sandbox environment — a practice copy of your portal where you can test changes without affecting production.

WHAT IS A SANDBOX?

Think of it as a "draft mode" for your HubSpot portal. You can break things, test migrations, try new workflows — all without affecting your real data or real customers. If something goes wrong, just delete the sandbox and start fresh.

HOW TO SET ONE UP
  1. Go to Settings (gear icon)
  2. Click Account Management in the left sidebar
  3. Click Sandboxes
  4. Click Create Sandbox
  5. Choose "Standard Sandbox" and give it a name (e.g., "Migration Test")
KEY FEATURES
FEATURE DETAIL
Deploy to Production Push tested changes (workflows, properties, forms) directly to your live portal
Configuration copy Sandbox copies your portal's setup (properties, pipelines, workflows) but starts with empty data
Isolation Nothing in the sandbox affects production — safe to experiment
Availability Enterprise tier only. Legacy sandboxes were sunset March 2026.
ALTERNATIVE: FREE DEVELOPER PORTAL

If you need additional testing space, you can create a free developer portal at developers.hubspot.com. It won't have Business Units (that requires Enterprise), but it's useful for testing API integrations and basic data flows.
