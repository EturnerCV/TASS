PHASE 2: CHOOSE TARGET PORTAL

WEEK 4 • 8 MIN READ

WHAT YOUR TEAM SHOULD EXPECT

A recommendation document will be presented with pros and cons of each target option. Leadership will need to make a decision — this determines the rest of the migration.

THE DECISION

This is the most important strategic decision of the entire migration: which portal becomes the permanent home for all your data? Every contact, deal, ticket, and workflow from every other portal will be migrated into this one.

There's no perfect answer — each option has trade-offs. The right choice depends on your specific data quality, team structure, and risk tolerance.

THREE OPTIONS

OPTION A

KEEP THE BIGGEST PORTAL

The portal with the most records stays put. Everything else migrates into it.

PROS

  • Least data to migrate
  • Email reputation preserved
  • Most users already set up

CONS

  • Might have the messiest data
  • Existing workflows may conflict
  • Legacy configuration baggage
OPTION B

KEEP THE BEST-CONFIGURED PORTAL

The portal with the cleanest data, best workflows, and strongest email reputation becomes home — even if it's not the biggest.

PROS

  • Cleanest foundation
  • Best workflows survive
  • Strongest email reputation

CONS

  • More data to migrate
  • Some users switch portals
  • Bigger migration window
OPTION C

FRESH ENTERPRISE PORTAL

Start from scratch. Create a new Enterprise portal and migrate everything from all existing portals into it.

PROS

  • Cleanest possible start
  • No legacy baggage
  • Everyone starts equal

CONS

  • ALL data must migrate
  • ALL workflows rebuilt
  • Email domains need full warm-up
  • Longest timeline

DECISION MATRIX

Score each portal 1-5 on these weighted criteria. The highest weighted total wins.

REAL-WORLD EXAMPLE

Company X had 3 portals. Portal A had 45,000 contacts but messy data — duplicate properties, broken workflows, inconsistent naming. Portal B had only 12,000 contacts but the cleanest workflows and strongest email reputation (3+ years of consistent sending). Portal C was small (5,000 contacts).

They chose Portal B as the target. The cleaner foundation was worth the larger migration from Portal A. The 3-year email reputation was especially valuable — it would have taken months to rebuild if they'd started fresh.

WHAT LEADERSHIP NEEDS TO DECIDE

  • Which portal becomes the target? Based on the decision matrix and team input.
  • What's the timeline? 30-90 days standard, but some organisations need more buffer.
  • Who leads the project? One person needs to own the migration end-to-end.
  • What's the budget? Migration tools, dedup tools, and potentially managed migration services.
