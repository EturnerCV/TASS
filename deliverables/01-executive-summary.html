THE PROBLEM

Your company runs multiple product lines, and each one has its own HubSpot portal. That seemed like a reasonable decision at the time. Each team got its own space to manage leads, run campaigns, and track deals without stepping on each other's toes.

But over time, this separation has created serious problems. The same customer who buys Product A and Product B exists as two completely separate records in two separate systems. Your marketing team sends duplicate emails because neither portal knows what the other one is doing. Your service team cannot see the full customer history when someone calls in with a problem. And your leadership team cannot get a single view of total revenue, customer lifetime value, or cross-sell performance because the data is locked inside separate silos.

You are also paying for the same contacts multiple times. HubSpot charges based on the number of marketing contacts in each portal. When the same person exists in three portals, you are billed three times for that one customer.

THE SOLUTION

Consolidate all portals into a single HubSpot Enterprise account with Business Units. Business Units is a HubSpot Enterprise feature that lets you run multiple brands from one portal. Each brand keeps its own identity (logos, email templates, landing pages, subscription preferences) while sharing a single CRM database underneath.

This means one contact record per customer, regardless of how many products they use. One complete timeline of every interaction. One subscription centre so customers never receive duplicate emails. And one set of reports that shows the full picture across all product lines.

THE OUTCOME

After consolidation, every team works from the same data. Sales can see cross-sell opportunities that were invisible before. Marketing stops sending conflicting messages to the same person. Service reps can see the full history without asking customers to repeat themselves. Leadership gets dashboards that show the real numbers, not a fragmented guess stitched together from separate exports.

The migration itself takes 6 to 8 weeks, phased carefully to avoid data loss, email deliverability issues, or disruption to your team's daily work. This guide walks through every step, from the initial audit to the final go-live.

KEY BENEFIT

Consolidation does not just clean up your tech stack. It unlocks revenue. When all customer data lives in one place, you can run cross-sell campaigns that were literally impossible before. You can identify your most valuable customers across all product lines. And you stop paying HubSpot for the same contact two, three, or four times over.

WHO THIS GUIDE IS FOR

This guide is written for the people who will decide whether to consolidate, and the people who will live with that decision every day. Each role gets something different out of this project.

CEO / FOUNDER

Unified Reporting

You get a single dashboard showing total revenue, customer lifetime value, and cross-sell performance across every product line. No more stitching together spreadsheets from separate portals to understand how the business is actually performing.

MARKETING DIRECTOR

Stop Duplicate Sends

One subscription centre means customers choose which brands they hear from. No more accidentally emailing the same person from two portals on the same day. Campaigns are filtered by brand, but powered by the full customer dataset.

SALES MANAGER

Cross-Sell Visibility

See which of your existing customers already use another product line. Identify warm cross-sell opportunities instead of cold-calling people who are already paying you. Every deal, across every product, on one contact timeline.

IT LEAD / OPS

Reduce Subscriptions

Manage one portal instead of three or four. One set of integrations. One set of users and permissions. One invoice. Less surface area for things to break, fewer systems to maintain, and lower total subscription costs.
