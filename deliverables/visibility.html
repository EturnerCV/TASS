03 — SEE CUSTOMERS

VISIBILITY

6 MIN READ

Before retention or upsell campaigns can work, the team has to be able to see the customer clearly. Today, that almost certainly means switching between four or five systems — finance, support ticketing, email, product analytics, a contract repository, and a spreadsheet or two — and mentally re-assembling the picture each time.

The CRM collapses that into a single record per customer, and four purpose-built views on top of it. Everything else in the business is a consumer of those views.

THE FOUNDATION

THE SINGLE CUSTOMER RECORD

One record per customer, holding everything. No more "which portal is that school in?" or "who's the champion again?" moments. Answers "what's the state of this account?" in 30 seconds, not a day of spreadsheet hunting.

FIELD SET 01 COMMERCIAL Contract status, MRR/ARR, tier, add-ons, renewal date, price history, payment method, invoice status, annual uplift history.
FIELD SET 02 PRODUCT Modules in use, feature flags, usage metrics, integration inventory, API call volume, environment (cloud vs on-prem), version.
FIELD SET 03 PEOPLE Main buyer, champion, daily users, IT contact, billing contact. Role changes tracked. LinkedIn-style notes on relationships.
FIELD SET 04 SUPPORT Ticket history, categories, escalations, CSAT scores, NPS responses, support-hour consumption, known issues tied to this account.
FIELD SET 05 COMMUNICATION Every email, call, meeting, QBR, survey response. Last-touched. Next-touch scheduled. Who spoke to them, about what.
FIELD SET 06 HEALTH Rolled-up risk score, expansion score, engagement trend. A single number for triage — backed by every signal underneath it.
THE CONSUMERS

FOUR VIEWS ON TOP

Each view is built for one decision-maker doing one job. Same underlying data, different lens.

V1
ACCOUNT MANAGER DASHBOARD
FOR THE AM TEAM

Prioritised next-action list per AM. Who to call today, why, and what to offer. Replaces "I think school X is due for a chat" with "here are your five highest-leverage calls this week, ranked by risk, opportunity, and relationship age."

Each card on the dashboard links directly to the customer record, opens a pre-filled call template, and logs the outcome back to the record when done. Two clicks, start to finish.

V2
RENEWAL PIPELINE
FOR SALES LEADERSHIP

Every contract date, price-review history, upsell conversation, and risk flag in one forward-looking view. 90 / 60 / 30 day buckets. Forecast at the top, drill-down to any specific account below.

Annual uplift becomes systematic rather than ad-hoc. Nothing expires quietly. Forecasts stop being a fiction-writing exercise.

V3
PORTFOLIO ROLL-UP
FOR LEADERSHIP + HEAD OFFICE

NRR, GRR, module penetration, cloud-vs-on-prem mix, support load per product line, win/loss reasons. The view head office asks for monthly, produced automatically from system-of-record data rather than assembled in Excel at 9pm the night before.

Drill-through to segment, to product, to single account. Same numbers everywhere — no more version-of-truth arguments.

V4
PRODUCT SIGNAL DASHBOARD
FOR THE ROADMAP TEAM

Ticket clusters, feature-request heat-map, module-adoption trend, competitive-loss reasons. Tells product where the base is complaining, asking, churning, and expanding. Feeds directly into the Ask Methodology for new-product discovery.

THE OUTCOME

SENIOR TIME BACK IN THE PRODUCTIVE COLUMN

Leaders and account managers stop doing work a system should do for them — pulling numbers, chasing updates, re-assembling customer history. That time gets reinvested in strategic accounts, expansion conversations, and the product roadmap.

More importantly: the whole business sees the same picture at the same time. Decisions get faster, better, and less dependent on whoever's closest to a given spreadsheet.
