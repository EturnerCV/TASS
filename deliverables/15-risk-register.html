CHAPTER 15

RISK REGISTER

6 MIN READ

Every migration has risks. The goal isn't to eliminate risk — it's to know what could go wrong and have a plan for each scenario. Here are the 8 risks we track for every portal consolidation.

RISK TABLE

RISKLIKELIHOODIMPACTMITIGATION
Duplicate contacts not fully resolved HIGH HIGH Run dedup tool before AND after migration. Use fuzzy matching (name + company + phone), not just email.
Email reputation drops (spam) MEDIUM CRITICAL Follow 4-week warm-up schedule strictly. Start with engaged contacts only. Monitor bounce and complaint rates daily.
Workflows fire incorrectly MEDIUM HIGH Migrate all workflows PAUSED. Activate one at a time, test with sample records, then move to the next.
Property type conflicts MEDIUM HIGH Complete the property mapping table before migration. Resolve all same-name-different-type conflicts in advance.
Integration failures MEDIUM MEDIUM Document all integrations. Reconnect and test each one. Allow 1-2 days for integration debugging.
Lost deal/ticket associations LOW CRITICAL Verify association counts pre vs post migration. Spot-check 20+ random records for correct links.
User adoption resistance MEDIUM MEDIUM Training before go-live (not after). Per-team "where to find your stuff" guides. Migration champion per team.
Temporary licensing cost spike HIGH LOW Budget for 2-3 months of overlap (old + new portals). Downgrade old portals to free tier after 90-day hold.

ROLLBACK PLAN

If something goes critically wrong during migration, here's the safety net:

ROLLBACK PROCEDURE

1. Stop all activity in the target portal — pause workflows, stop email sends.
2. Re-activate source portals — they should still be intact and read-only.
3. Resume operations from source portals — teams go back to their original portals.
4. Diagnose the issue — what went wrong? Data? Workflows? Email?
5. Fix in sandbox — resolve the issue, test the fix.
6. Retry migration — run again with the fix in place.

This is why source portals are kept read-only for 90 days — they're your safety net. Never delete source portal data during or immediately after migration.
