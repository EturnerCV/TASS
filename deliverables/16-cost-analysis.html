WORKED EXAMPLE

Here's how the math works for a typical 3-portal consolidation:

BEFORE: 3 PORTALS

  • 3x Marketing Hub Pro @ $890/mo = $2,670/mo
  • 6 Sales seats across portals @ $100 = $600/mo
  • 4 Service seats across portals @ $100 = $400/mo
  • 18,000 total contacts (3,200 duplicates) charged at higher tier
  • Total: ~$3,970/mo

AFTER: 1 PORTAL

  • 1x Marketing Hub Enterprise @ $3,600/mo
  • 4 Sales seats (deduplicated) @ $150 = $600/mo
  • 3 Service seats (deduplicated) @ $150 = $450/mo
  • 14,800 unique contacts — lower tier, charged once
  • Total: ~$4,650/mo + BU add-on

"Wait — the Enterprise portal costs more?"

At face value, yes — the base subscription is higher. But factor in: contact tier savings from eliminating 3,200 duplicates, the cross-sell revenue you're currently missing (even a 5% cross-sell rate on 3,200 overlapping customers generates significant revenue), unified reporting that catches problems earlier, and the operational time saved by teams not managing 3 separate systems. The ROI isn't just in licensing savings — it's in revenue you're leaving on the table.

EXPECTED ROI

Companies with significant customer overlap (20%+ contacts appearing in multiple portals) typically see positive ROI within 3-6 months when factoring in licensing savings, reduced duplicate work, and cross-sell revenue. The migration investment is a one-time cost; the savings are permanent.

NEGOTIATION TIPS

  • Bundle discount: HubSpot offers a "Customer Platform" bundle that discounts all hubs purchased together. Ask about this when upgrading to Enterprise.
  • Migration support: Ask HubSpot for migration support credits — they have an interest in you consolidating (happier customer, less churn risk).
  • Annual commitment: Annual prepay typically saves 10-20% vs monthly billing.
  • Timing: End of quarter is when HubSpot reps are most flexible on pricing.
