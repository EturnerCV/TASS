CHAPTER 16

COST ANALYSIS

6 MIN READ

Consolidation is not a cost-cutting exercise. It's a revenue and visibility exercise. The licensing cost may stay similar or even increase — but the value you get from unified data, cross-sell capability, and operational efficiency makes the current multi-portal setup the more expensive option by far.

WHERE THE REAL COST IS TODAY

The cost of running separate portals isn't just the subscription fees. It's the invisible costs that don't show up on an invoice:

HIDDEN COST

DUPLICATE CONTACT CHARGES

Every customer who appears in multiple portals is being paid for multiple times. HubSpot charges based on marketing contact tiers. If 20% of your contacts exist across 3 portals, you're paying for those people three times over.

HIDDEN COST

MISSED CROSS-SELL REVENUE

You cannot run "customers who bought Product A but not Product B" campaigns because the data lives in separate databases. Every month without cross-sell visibility is revenue left on the table. Even a modest 5% cross-sell conversion rate on overlapping customers generates significant recurring revenue.

HIDDEN COST

OPERATIONAL OVERHEAD

Three portals means three sets of workflows to maintain, three sets of reports to build, three integrations to manage, three email domains to monitor. Your teams are doing the same work three times. After consolidation, they do it once.

HIDDEN COST

POOR CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE

Customers getting bombarded with emails from multiple portals unsubscribe, mark as spam, or churn. Each lost customer has a lifetime value that dwarfs any subscription saving. One frustrated customer who leaves because "they keep emailing me from three different accounts" costs more than a year of Enterprise licensing.

WHAT CONSOLIDATION GIVES YOU

The value isn't in paying less — it's in getting more from what you pay for:

  • One invoice — finance deals with one vendor relationship, not three
  • Contact deduplication — pay once per customer, not three times
  • Cross-sell revenue — campaigns that were impossible before now generate new revenue
  • Unified reporting — true customer count, real LTV, accurate forecasting
  • Reduced IT maintenance — one set of integrations, one set of workflows
  • Better customer experience — one subscription centre, frequency capping, full service history
  • AI-powered operations — one MCP connection gives every team instant access to complete data
THE BOTTOM LINE

The exact cost comparison depends on your specific portal configurations, contact volumes, and seat counts — this is something the HubSpot Partner calculates during the audit phase with real numbers from your account. What's universally true: the hidden costs of fragmented data, missed revenue, and operational duplication always exceed the licensing difference.

NEGOTIATION TIPS

Your HubSpot Partner handles pricing negotiation — but here's what to know going in:

  • Bundle discount: HubSpot offers a "Customer Platform" bundle that discounts all hubs purchased together. This is where Enterprise becomes competitive — the bundle price is significantly lower than buying hubs individually.
  • Migration credits: HubSpot funds migration support for partner-led projects. Your partner can request these on your behalf — this can significantly reduce or eliminate migration tool costs.
  • Annual commitment: Annual prepay typically saves 10-20% vs monthly billing.
  • Timing: End of quarter is when HubSpot reps are most flexible on pricing. Plan your purchase accordingly.
  • Consolidation leverage: You're going from 3-4 subscriptions to 1. HubSpot would rather keep you as a customer on Enterprise than lose you entirely. Use this leverage.
