CHAPTER 05

PROFESSIONAL VS ENTERPRISE

8 MIN READ

THE KEY QUESTION

Before you can consolidate your portals, you need to decide which HubSpot tier to run on. This is not just a pricing decision. The tier you choose determines whether key consolidation features are even available to you.

The two realistic options are Professional and Enterprise. Starter is too limited for multi-brand operations, and Free is not a serious option for a business of this size. So the real question is: do you need the features that only come with Enterprise, or can you work around their absence on Professional?

This chapter gives you a clear, honest comparison so you can make that decision with confidence.

ENTERPRISE-ONLY FEATURES EXPLAINED

Here are the six Enterprise-only features and why each one matters for a multi-brand consolidation.

BUSINESS UNITS

This is the cornerstone of the entire consolidation. Business Units let you run multiple brands from one portal. Each brand gets its own logos, email templates, forms, landing pages, subscription preferences, and reporting, while sharing one CRM database underneath. Without Business Units, you cannot properly separate brands within a single portal. This feature alone is the primary reason most multi-brand companies need Enterprise.

CUSTOM OBJECTS

HubSpot comes with standard objects like Contacts, Companies, Deals, and Tickets. Custom Objects let you create entirely new types of records that are specific to your business. For example, if you track "Locations" or "Subscriptions" or "Projects" as separate entities linked to contacts, you need Custom Objects. Enterprise gives you up to 10 custom object definitions. Professional has zero.

SANDBOX

A Sandbox is a safe copy of your portal where you can test changes without affecting production. For a migration of this scale, being able to run a full test migration in a sandbox before touching real data is extremely valuable. It is the difference between "we tested everything first" and "we hope this works." Enterprise includes a Standard Sandbox. Professional has no sandbox at all.

ADVANCED PERMISSIONS

With multiple brands in one portal, you need fine-grained control over who can see and edit what. Advanced permissions let you restrict access by team, by brand, by pipeline, and by record type. For example, the Product A sales team can be restricted to only see Product A deals, while leadership sees everything. Professional only offers basic permission sets that are not granular enough for multi-brand operations.

TEAM HIERARCHY

Team hierarchy lets you create nested team structures. A "Product A" parent team can have "Product A Sales" and "Product A Support" child teams underneath it. Permissions flow down the hierarchy, which makes managing access across brands much simpler. Professional gives you flat teams with no nesting, and only 10 of them. Enterprise gives you 300 teams with full hierarchy.

PREDICTIVE SCORING

Predictive lead scoring uses machine learning to automatically score leads based on their likelihood to close. With all your brands in one portal, the scoring model has access to a much larger dataset. It can identify patterns that would be invisible when data is split across separate portals. For example, it might discover that customers who buy Product A are highly likely to also buy Product C. Professional only offers manual scoring, which requires you to define every rule yourself.

CAN YOU DO THIS ON PROFESSIONAL?

Honestly, yes, partially. But it requires significant manual workarounds, and some capabilities are simply not available.

Manual tagging workaround: Instead of Business Units, you would create a custom contact property (a multiple-checkbox field) called something like "Brand" with one option per product line. You would manually tag every contact. Marketing would need to filter every campaign, every list, and every report by this property. It works, but it is entirely manual. There is no automatic brand separation for email templates, forms, landing pages, or subscription preferences. Every team member has to remember to apply the right filter every time.

Developer portal instead of sandbox: You can create a free developer portal at developers.hubspot.com for basic testing. However, it will not have Business Units or your actual portal configuration, so you cannot run a realistic test migration. You are testing in a fundamentally different environment than your production portal.

No custom objects: If your business needs custom data types beyond Contacts, Companies, Deals, and Tickets, Professional cannot accommodate that. There is no workaround. You would need to store that data outside HubSpot and sync it in, which adds complexity and fragility.

THE HONEST ASSESSMENT

Professional works for 2 brands with simple structures and limited overlap between customer bases. Once you have 3 or more brands with overlapping customers who buy across product lines, the manual workarounds break down. Enterprise is the right choice for that scenario.

TOTAL COST COMPARISON

This is where it gets interesting. Enterprise costs more per month, but you are paying for one portal instead of three. When you factor in duplicate contact charges and multiple subscriptions, Enterprise often costs less than running multiple Professional portals.

COST ITEM 3x PROFESSIONAL 1x ENTERPRISE
Marketing Hub subscription $890 x 3 = $2,670/mo $3,600/mo
Sales seats (assume 3 reps) $100 x 3 seats x 3 portals = $900/mo $150 x 3 seats = $450/mo
Service seats (assume 2 reps) $100 x 2 seats x 3 portals = $600/mo $150 x 2 seats = $300/mo
Business Units add-on Not available ~$500/mo (varies by contract)
Duplicate contact charges Paying for the same contacts 2-3 times across portals Each contact counted once
ESTIMATED MONTHLY TOTAL $4,170/mo + duplicate contact fees $4,850/mo, no duplicates

At first glance, Enterprise looks $680/mo more expensive. But that does not include the duplicate contact fees you are paying across three Professional portals. If even 25% of your contacts exist in more than one portal, the duplicate charges alone can exceed that $680 difference. And that is before you factor in the time your team wastes managing three separate systems, the missed cross-sell revenue, and the cost of duplicate marketing emails going to the same customers.

The real cost comparison is not "$4,170 vs $4,850." It is "total cost of three separate portals with duplicates, wasted time, and missed revenue" vs "$4,850 with a single clean system." When you factor everything in, Enterprise typically saves money within the first 3-6 months.

RECOMMENDATION

OUR RECOMMENDATION

For a company running 3-4 product lines with overlapping customers, HubSpot Enterprise with Business Units is the right choice. It is the only tier that provides native brand separation, sandbox testing for safe migration, advanced permissions for controlling access across brands, and the deduplication capacity needed to merge multiple portals cleanly. The initial sticker price is higher, but the total cost of ownership is lower once you eliminate duplicate subscriptions, duplicate contact charges, and the hidden cost of teams working in separate systems.
